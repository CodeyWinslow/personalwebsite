BACKGROUND

Hello! I am honored to see you here! I am currently an Oregon Tech student in the Computer Software Engineering Technology program working for my first bachelor's degree, and I am looking for a summer internship.

I was 11 when I started making video games, and I immediately fell in love with it. A program called Game Maker 8 introduced me and gave me the tools to explore a different side of a beloved medium I had grown up on. I didn't initially enjoy programming because of how intimidating it was at the time, but when faced with a problem that required a simple form of scripting, I fell in love with that, too! Since then, I have consistently built my programming skills to set myself apart from the vast body of people who have the immense passion for video games that I do.

My first two years after high school was spent at Umpqua Community College where I enjoyed every bit of earning an Associate of Science degree in Computer Information Systems. I learned many valuable skills and a substantial amound of knowledge in varying areas of technology, but I didn't get the focus on software or games that I craved. As fate would have it, the Oregon Institute of Technology fell right in front of me and I seized the opportunity.

I am now a Junior in the sofware engineering program at OIT, and I could not have anticipated the skills and knowledge I have acquired this far. The progress I have made at Oregon Tech has helped ensure that whatever team I am part of knows they can count on me to make quality work, communicate efficiently, and understand my team members in a way that makes the workplace productive and enjoyable!

PERSONALITY

I am an inquisitive person. I like to understand how everything works because the world is fascinating. If I had it my way, I would be an expert in everything even if I had to live a thousand lifetimes. This lifetime, however, I have chosen to be an expert in software.

Although most of my life I have considered myself to be an overly structure-oriented person, I have discovered the value of molding myself into a more well-rounded version. I truly admire every aspect of game development. From the game / level / character design and asset creation to the complex and genius engineering that goes into graphics, in-game systems, and gameplay mechanics. This admiration manifests itself into the passion that drives me to be best I can be, and to one day deliver games that make people feel the way I did as a child.

If I had to pick one trait that sets me apart from most people you (yes, you, the reader) have met, it would be compassion. I spend close to half of my time emulating the perspectives of others, or at least bettering myself by understanding their perspectives. In many ways, this influences me to be hypercritical about myself, but I embrace this by using it to be self-aware.

If you want one thing to remember me by, know that not only my skills, but also my presence would make a lasting impact on your project and your team!

WHY HIRE ME?

Other than my claim that I am good at what I do, I think you would just enjoy having me around!

In case the "Personality" section seems to exhaustive, I'll summarize it here: I strive to be a well-rounded person, and along with my technical skills, I empathize well with others.

In an ever-growing market of software engineers, it would be untruthful to say skilled programmers are hard to come by. While many of my colleagues have put all of their efforts into building technical skills, I have chosen to sacrifice some of that time to build myself as a person. I think you will find not only my technical ability to be desirable, but you'll also find my personability to be a superb addition to your team.
